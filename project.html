Introduction

There has been significant improvement in girls' primary education worldwide since 2000. Morocco has shown the most dramatic improvement, while Chad is the poorest performer. However, most countries are improving, as the chart below shows.

Note that in the chart above, the rate is calculated as the number of entrants into the last year of primary education over the population of children at the entrance age for the last year of primary education; this returns some values above 100% where there are children starting the school year at a younger or older age.
